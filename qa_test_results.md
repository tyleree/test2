### Question 3: CARDIO-3

**Query:** How is high blood pressure rated by the VA?

**Category:** CARDIO

**Response Time:** 0.13s

**Answer:**

High blood pressure (hypertension, isolated systolic hypertension, hypertensive vascular disease) is rated by the VA based on blood pressure readings. Here are the key points for rating:

- Blood pressure readings must be taken two or more times on at least three different days to properly diagnose hypertension.
- Veterans can receive a separate rating for hypertension even if they have hypertensive heart disease.
- Veterans with hypertension due to aortic insufficiency or hyperthyroidism will not receive a separate rating for hypertension.
- Hypertension can be considered a presumptive condition if:
  - It is diagnosed within a year of separation and was at least 10% disabling within that time,
  - The veteran was exposed to Agent Orange,
  - The veteran was a POW and the severity was at least 10% at some point.
- There is also quasi presumptive service-connection for veterans who develop hypertension after certain amputations.

The specific rating percentages and criteria are not fully detailed in the provided context, but the process and requirements for diagnosis and rating eligibility are outlined¹.

### Question 8: MENTAL-3

**Query:** What are the rating criteria for depression?

**Category:** MENTAL

**Response Time:** 0.16s

**Answer:**

The rating criteria for depression (and other mental health disorders) are based on the severity of the totality of a veteran's symptoms, not the specific diagnosis. Veterans receive a single rating regardless of the number of mental health diagnoses they have. The rating is determined by how closely the veteran's symptoms match the criteria for each percentage level, considering all evidence submitted, such as examiner's reports, personal statements, and treatment notes.

For example, the 50% rating criteria for depression (and other mental disorders) are:

- Occupational and social impairment with reduced reliability and productivity due to such symptoms as:
  - Flattened affect
  - Circumstantial, circumlocutory, or stereotyped speech
  - Panic attacks more than once a week
  - Difficulty in understanding complex commands
  - Impairment of short- and long-term memory (e.g., retention of only highly learned material, forgetting to complete tasks)
  - Impaired judgment
  - Impaired abstract thinking
  - Disturbances of motivation and mood
  - Difficulty in establishing and maintaining effective work and social relationships

Additional notes for the 50% rating:
- Mostly able to care for themselves, though hygiene may not always be maintained
- Able to do daily tasks (driving, shopping, chores), but may occasionally be hospitalized
- Can work, but may have difficulty staying employed for long periods
- May be able to form relationships, but they may be short-lived due to difficulty being an active participant
- Symptoms may include extreme lack of motivation, mood swings, inappropriate emotional responses, frequent panic attacks, severe anxiety, difficulty with complex directions, and poor judgment³

The rating is ultimately based on the totality of symptoms and how closely they match the criteria for each percentage level, not just the presence of a diagnosis like depression.

### Question 12: MSK-2

**Query:** How are knee conditions rated by the VA?

**Category:** MSK

**Response Time:** 0.13s

**Answer:**

Knee conditions are rated by the VA primarily based on range of motion (ROM). The examiner and rater are required to consider flare-ups in your condition, meaning they must estimate your impairment as it would be on your worst day, even if your examination does not occur during a flare-up. The most impaired ROM measurements noted by the examiner must be used for rating purposes¹.

If arthritis is present in the knee and does not warrant a rating based on ROM, the condition will be rated under degenerative arthritis criteria. For degenerative arthritis, there must be x-ray evidence to confirm the diagnosis. Ratings for degenerative arthritis are as follows:

- 10%: If 2 or more major joints (including the knee) or 2 or more minor joint groups are affected³.

If the knee condition is due to certain diseases (like bursitis, gout, or tendinitis), these are rated only on how they affect range of motion and do not receive the major/minor joint treatment².

### Question 15: MSK-5

**Query:** What are the rating criteria for degenerative disc disease?

**Category:** MSK

**Response Time:** 0.13s

**Answer:**

Degenerative Disc Disease (DDD) can be rated under two main criteria based on the context provided:

### 1. Incapacitating Episodes (DC 5243)
If a Veteran with DDD (also known as Intervertebral Disc Syndrome or IVDS) has incapacitating episodes requiring **prescribed** bed rest within a 12-month period, the following ratings apply:

- **10%**: At least 1 but less than 2 weeks of prescribed bed rest
- **20%**: At least 2 but less than 4 weeks
- **40%**: At least 4 but less than 6 weeks
- **60%**: More than 6 weeks

**Note:** The Veteran can be rated based on either incapacitating episodes or range of motion (ROM), but not both. The higher rating is used. If rated under this schedule, separate ratings for related spine nerve conditions are **not** allowed¹.

### 2. Limitations of Range of Motion (ROM) (DC 5235–5242)
If the Veteran does not meet the criteria for incapacitating episodes, DDD may be rated based on limitation of motion of the spine. Only one rating per section of the spine (neck or middle/lower back) is allowed, even if multiple conditions affect the same section³.

#### Thoracolumbar (Back) Ratings:
- **0%**: Forward flexion ≥ 90°, or combined ROM ≥ 240°
- **10%**: Forward flexion > 60° but ≤ 85°, or combined ROM 125°–240°
- (Further criteria are truncated in the context)

#### Cervical (Neck) Ratings:
- **0%**: Forward flexion ≥ 45°, or combined ROM ≥ 340°
- **10%**: Forward flexion > 30° but < 45°, or combined ROM 175°–340°
- (Further criteria are truncated in the context)

**Note:** Only the most impaired ROM measurements should be used, including estimates for flare-ups if not directly observed during examination⁶.

### Additional Notes:
- DDD is **not** considered arthritis and is not subject to presumptive service connection for arthritis².
- If a Veteran qualifies for both ROM and incapacitating episode ratings, only the higher rating is applied, not both¹ ⁷.

### Question 17: RESP-2

**Query:** How is sleep apnea rated by the VA?

**Category:** RESP

**Response Time:** 0.13s

**Answer:**

Sleep apnea is rated by the VA under Diagnostic Code 6847. The ratings are based on the severity of symptoms and the need for breathing assistance devices. Here are the rating criteria:

- **0%**: No symptoms, but there is a diagnosis.
- **30%**: Persistent sleepiness during the daytime or not feeling rested after sleeping.
- **50%**: Requires use of a breathing assistance device such as a CPAP machine or similar devices (e.g., APAP, BiPAP, NIPPV, NIV, nasopharyngeal appliances, oral appliances, or implanted genioglossal nerve stimulation devices).

**Important notes:**
- The VA must have a sleep study to grant service connection for sleep apnea. Home sleep studies are acceptable if a competent medical provider has evaluated the results and determined they are appropriate.
- The veteran only needs to be prescribed a CPAP (or similar device) to qualify for the 50% rating; actual use is not required to maintain this rating¹.

### Question 31: 1151-4

**Query:** How do I file an 1151 claim?

**Category:** 1151

**Response Time:** 0.13s

**Answer:**

To file an 1151 claim, you need to:

- Identify your claim as a 1151 claim when filing your VA disability claim. There is no special or separate form for a 1151 claim¹.
- Provide information to help the VA obtain relevant records, including:
  - Where the event occurred
  - Approximately when the event occurred
  - An explanation of how the provider made a mistake¹
- Your claim should include:
  1. An event that meets the 1151 criteria (such as an injury or aggravation caused by VA medical care).
  2. Evidence that a disability or death began or was made worse after the event.

When filing, make sure to clearly state that your claim is under 1151 and provide all the required details and supporting evidence.
